Camel

Some Facts

A camel is an even-toed ungulate in the genus

Camelus that bears distinctive fatty deposits known

"humps" on its back. Camels have long been

domesticated and, as livestock, they provide food and textiles.

Chameleon

Some Facts

Chameleons or chamaeleons are a distinctive and

highly specialized clade of Old World lizards with 202

described as of June 2015. These species

in a range of colors, and many species have the

to change color.

Pigeon

Some Facts

Columbidae is a bird family consisting of pigeons and

doves. It is the only family in the order

Columbiformes. These are stout-bodied birds with

short necks, and short slender bills that in some

species feature fleshy ceres. They primarily feed on

seeds, fruits, and plants.


Bear

Some Facts

Bears are carnivoran mammals of the family Ursidae.

They are classified as caniforms, or doglike

carnivorans. Although only eight species of bears are

extant, they are widespread, appearing in a wide

variety of habitats throughout the Northern

Hemisphere and partially in the Southern Hemisphere.

Panda

Some Facts

The giant panda, also known as the panda bear or

simply the panda, is a bear native to South Central

China. It is characterised by its bold black-and-white

coat and rotund body. The name "giant panda" is

sometimes used to distinguish it from the red panda,

a neighboring musteloid.
